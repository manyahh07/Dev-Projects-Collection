What is Sorting?

Sorting is the process of rearranging elements into a specific order — ascending, descending, or by custom criteria. It's one of the most studied problems in computer science, forming the backbone of databases, search engines, and countless real-world applications.

Algorithms split into two families: Comparison Sorts (Bubble, Selection, Insertion, Merge, Quick, Heap) which work by comparing elements head-to-head — bounded by O(n log n) in theory — and Non-Comparison Sorts (Counting, Radix, Bucket) which exploit element structure to achieve linear O(n) time under the right conditions.
